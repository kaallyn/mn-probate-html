    • Disposition Options

    When dealing with a property as part of an estate, there are five main ways to handle it. Here are the areas in general and some pros and cons of each.

    1. Keep the property

    Pros:

    • Easy to do if property is already an income producing property
    • Generates income for the estate while rest of the estate is settled
    • Don’t have to worry about removing tenant in the short term

    Cons:

    • Someone has to manage it or estate has to pay for a management company
    • Eventually it will have to be valued and sold to another investor or owner occupant so the estate can be closed

    2. Sell property to family member

    Pros:

    • Might be excluded from some property disclosure and TISH / POS requirements
    • Avoid many transactional costs

    Cons:

    • Might cause some stress with family members who did not get the house
    • PR might feel the need to discount the price because of family and that might be against the fiduciary duties

    3. Sell property in “as-is” condition

    Pros:

    • Quickest access to house equity
    • Least amount of time and effort prior to sale

    Cons:

    • Typically lowest price received
    • Smaller buyer pool

    4. Sell property after some updates

    Pros:

    • Balances prep time and equity received for the estate
    • Largest buyer pool

    Cons:

    • Need a budget to pay for updates
    • Need access to trust, qualified remodelers

    5. Sell property after full renovation

    Pros:

    • Probable highest sales price

    Cons:

    • Highest amount of work for the estate
    • If not done properly, it could drain the estate.
